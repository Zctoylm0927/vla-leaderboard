Characterizing Vision-Language-Action Models across XPUs

Constraints and acceleration for low-cost, energy-aware, real-time on-robot deployment.

Kaijun Zhou, Qiwei Chen, Da Peng, Zhiyang Li, Xijun Li, Jinyu Gu · Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Paper Overview

We study VLA deployment as a model-hardware co-design problem: the right platform depends on latency feasibility, acquisition cost, energy budget, memory capacity, and the structure of the model pipeline.

Key Findings

The evaluation explains why peak FLOPS alone is a weak deployment guide for embodied VLA workloads.

Finding 1

Model size does not predict speed

Fewer parameters do not always mean higher inference frequency, because iterative Action Expert denoising can dominate latency.

Finding 2

Hardware choice is metric-dependent

Latency, cost, energy, CE, and CET rankings can select different platforms for the same VLA workload.

Finding 3

VLA execution is imbalanced

The workflow has a dual-phase imbalance: the VLM backbone uses hardware well, while the Action Expert often dominates latency.

Finding 4

Robotics inverts bottlenecks

Unlike typical generation workloads, the VLM backbone is compute-bound while the Action Expert is memory-bound.

Finding 5

Optimizations must compose globally

Phase-aware optimizations such as compilation, DP-Cache, and V-AEFusion need to be combined with an end-to-end view.

Finding 6

Optimization gains vary by hardware

The same method can speed up one platform but offer limited benefit on another due to hardware design and resource contention.

Acceleration Methods

The optimizations target the two serialization points observed in VLA inference: repeated denoising inside the Action Expert and synchronous VLM-to-Action-Expert execution.

Compilation

Hardware-aware execution

torch.compile and Ascend ATC reduce framework overhead and fuse operations, with platform-dependent gains.

DP-Cache

Skip redundant diffusion steps

Offline profiling identifies stable diffusion segments, then cached intermediate results bypass redundant computation during inference.

V-AEFusion

Overlap VLM and Action Expert

Early denoising uses stale but temporally coherent visual features while the VLM processes the current observation.
